Artist Bio

John creates intimate, small-scale watercolor paintings that feel like quiet gifts from another world. Working at a miniature size invites closeness, each piece asking the viewer to lean in, slow down, and share a gentle moment of attention. Though small in scale, the paintings carry emotional weight, often capturing wonder, softness, and fleeting beauty in just a few inches of space.

John lives and creates with Tourette's Syndrome, ADHD, and OCD, and these neurological rhythms are not separate from the art, they are woven into its making. A unique motor tic affects his right hand specifically when he begins to paint or draw. Where many would be forced to stop, John has developed a practice of painting through the movement. The act of creating becomes a point of focus strong enough to quiet the urge to put the brush down. The result is work that holds a subtle energy, a living line, a human vibration, that cannot be replicated by stillness alone.

Each tiny painting stands as a small act of persistence and generosity. They are reminders that beauty can come from working with the body rather than against it, and that art can be both a grounding force and a quiet triumph.
